Period in review: July 24 - 31, 2007

Japan

(

Jul 30, 2007

)

The official results of the Parliamentary elections gave a hard blow to PM Shinzo ABE’s Liberal Democratic Party (LDP). The LDP and its ally the New Komeito (NK) party received only 37 and 9 seats respectively, falling short of the 64 seats needed to maintain their majority in the 242 seat House of Councilors. On the other hand, the Democratic Party of Japan (DPJ) won 60 seats in this election, thus giving the opposition a majority of 140 seats. Despite the defeat, Prime Minister ABE said he would not resign.

Mali

(

Jul 25, 2007

)

According to the provisional results released on July 24, the ruling Alliance for Democracy and Progress (ADP) received 128 of the 147 seats in the National Assembly, one third more than in the last elections. The leading opposition group, the Front for Democracy and the Republic (FDR) took just 15 seats as opposed to 50 in the 2002 legislative elections. The remainder of the four seats went to the African Solidarity for Democracy and Independence (SADI) party. Even though the West African Economic and Monetary Union (UEMOA) and the 900 election observers overlooking the polls did not report any major irregularities, the results will be official only after the Constitutional Court validates them. Furthermore, despite efforts by the authorities and NGOs to convince citizens to cast their vote, turnout was as low as 33%.

Vietnam

(

Jul 24, 2007

)

The Vietnamese National Assembly has re-elected current president Nguyen Minh TRIET for another term in office. The Communist Party of Vietnam Central Committee (CPVCC) representative received an overwhelming 487 of the 493 votes of the National Assembly.
