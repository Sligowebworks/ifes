Week in review: September 1-7, 2006

Mexico

(

Sep 06, 2006

)

The Federal Electoral Tribunal declared conservative Felipe CALDERON president-elect, two months after Mexicans cast their vote. The court rejected allegations of fraud and ruled out that Felipe CALDERON won the July 2 election by 233,831 votes, or less than 1% of the 41 million votes cast. His opponent, leftist Andres Manuel Lopez OBRADOR refused to accept the tribunal's ruling and said he would not recognize the new government. The decision was announced after two months of investigation into allegations of fraud and after a partial recount of the vote. In the meantime, mass protests staged by Lopez OBRADOR's supporters disrupted the capital. Under Mexican law, the president is elected by plurality and no run-off election can be organized to decide on a finalist. Felipe CALDERON is expected to take office on December 1, 2006.

Guyana

(

Sep 01, 2006

)

According to results announced by the election commission on August 30, the People's Progressive Party/Civic (PPP/C) won the August 28 general elections with 54.6% of the vote and secured 36 out of the 65 contested parliamentary seats. As leader of the winning party, incumbent president Bharrat JAGDEO automatically qualified for a second term in office. Further seats in the parliament were secured by the People's National Congress/Reform – One Guyana (PNC/R-1G) which enjoyed the support of 34% of voters, and the Alliance For Change (AFC) with 8.4%. The PNC/R-1G took 22 seats and the AFC 5. The remaining two seats were split between the Guyana Action Party/Rise Organize and Rebuild Guyana (GAP/ROAR) and The United Force (TUF). The Justice For All Party (JFAP) did not obtain enough votes to enter the parliament.
