Panama

(

Oct 25, 2006

)

According to complete results released by the Electoral Court, 705,144 Panamanians (77.80%) have supported the Panama Canal Authority's project to build a third set of locks in the canal connecting the Pacific and the Atlantic Oceans. A total of 201,247 Panamanians (22.20%) voted against the project. Voter turnout was relatively low at 43.32%.

Bulgaria

(

Oct 24, 2006

)

Despite obtaining most of the votes in the October 22 election, incumbent President Georgi PARVANOV failed to win an outright victory due to low voter turnout. Under Bulgarian electoral law, at least 50% of registered voters must vote for a candidate to win an outright victory in the first round. Final results announced by the Central Election Commission gave PARVANOV 1,780,119 votes (64%) and his main rival, Attack leader Volen SIDEROV, 597,175 (21.5%). Nedelcho BERONOV came in third with 21.5% of votes and was followed by Georgi MARKOV with 2.7%. A run-off election between PARVANOV and SIDEROV is scheduled for October 29.

France

(

Oct 22, 2006

)

The French government has set April 22, 2007 as the date of the next presidential election. In the eventuality that no candidate wins a majority of the vote, a run-off will be held on May 6 between the top two candidates in the first round. The presidential campaign will start on April 9. Parliamentary elections have been scheduled for June 10 and 17, 2007.
